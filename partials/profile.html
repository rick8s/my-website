Following an enlistment in the U.S. Navy, I worked for General Motors for thirty years. It wasn't until more recently that I became interested in software as a career.

While volunteering on the board of East Williamson Athletic Association I decided to take a class in web development because we needed a new website. Building the EWA site led to building several additional sites including a youth travel baseball team, a small consignment business - Rocking Horse Consignments - and a site for Fairways of Spencer Creek, a golf course in Franklin. While these sites were not very complex, they did serve to inspire me to want to learn more.

In 2009 I returned to school and earned a Bachelor of Science in Computer Information Systems. It was at this time that I decided I would like to develop software professionally. In 2015 I left General Motors and a week later began the next chapter in my life at Nashville Software School. Here, I have been introduced to the foundational tools that I can now build upon. I have thoroughly enjoyed this challenging environment, and I am looking forward to the continual development of my skills in the workforce.

Married with two children, I have lived in the Nashville area for the past twenty-six years and currently reside in Franklin, Tn.
